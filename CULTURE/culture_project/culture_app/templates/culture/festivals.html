Major Festivals of India

Diwali

Known as the Festival of Lights, Diwali symbolizes the victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and hope over despair.

Holi

Holi, the Festival of Colors, marks the arrival of spring and the victory of good over evil. It is celebrated with joy, colors, and dance.

Eid

Eid is a significant Islamic festival that marks the end of Ramadan. It is celebrated with prayers, feasts, and a spirit of brotherhood.

Christmas

Christmas celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ. It is a time of joy, gift-giving, and sharing love with friends and family.

Navratri

Navratri is a nine-night festival dedicated to Goddess Durga. It is celebrated with dance, music, and devotion across India.

Pongal

Pongal is a harvest festival celebrated in Tamil Nadu. It is a thanksgiving to the Sun God for agricultural abundance.

Raksha Bandhan

Raksha Bandhan is a traditional festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters, where sisters tie a thread on brothers wrist.

Makar Sankranti

Makar Sankranti is Hindu festival celebrated on 14 january.It symbolizes the beginning of longer days and harvest season.
