Classical and Folk Music & Dance of India

Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is a classical dance form originating from Tamil Nadu, known for its grace, expressions, and intricate footwork.

Kathak

Kathak is a classical dance form from North India, characterized by intricate footwork, spins, and expressive storytelling.

Sitar

The sitar is a stringed instrument widely used in Indian classical music, known for its melodic and resonant sound.

Bhangra

Bhangra is a lively folk dance from Punjab, performed to the energetic beats of the dhol, celebrating joy and harvest.

Tabla

The tabla is a pair of drums used in Hindustani classical music, known for its complex rhythms and versatile sound.

Odissi

Odissi is one of the oldest classical dance forms from Odisha, characterized by its fluid movements, sculpturesque poses, and emotive expressions.

Bihu

Bihu is a vibrant and joyous festival celebrated in the northeastern state of Assam, India. It marks the Assamese New Year and is closely associated with agriculture, especially the harvest season.

Keerthana

Keerthana (also spelled Kirtana or Keertana) is a form of devotional music in Carnatic music tradition, primarily sung in praise of gods and goddesses in South India.
